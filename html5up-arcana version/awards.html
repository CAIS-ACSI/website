Student-to-CAIS award

Purpose

The Student-to-CAIS award is given annually to the best scholarly paper submitted by a student and accepted for presentation at the CAIS annual conference.

Nature of the award

The award consists of a $1,000 (Canadian currency) prize which, in the case of a jointly authored submission, will be shared equally among the authors.

Conditions

Abstracts are adjudicated by the CAIS/ACSI Conference Program Committee. The winning author(s) must attend CAIS/ACSI to give their paper and receive the award.

Nomination

To be considered for this award the abstract must represent a report of scholarly research. Jointly authored submissions are eligible so long as all authors were students at the time when the research described in the abstract was completed. While this competition will be of particular interest to graduate students (both doctoral and masters), it is open to all students of all ages and levels, both full and part time. The competition is open to both Canadian and international students.

Selection

All student abstracts submitted will be considered for this award as long as they meet the criteria. Should the CAIS/ACSI Conference Program Committee determine that no submission is worthy, an award will not be given.
